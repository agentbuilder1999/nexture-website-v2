### FAQ Section

**Section Headline**:
Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Is TheraSeus FDA-approved?**
A: TheraSeus is not yet FDA-approved. We are currently operating under NZ medical device regulations (Medsafe SaMD classification pending) and preparing our FDA 510(k) or De Novo submission for 2027. In the meantime, TheraSeus is available for pilot use and non-diagnostic applications in New Zealand.

**Q2: Does TheraSeus replace the doctor?**
A: No. TheraSeus is an AI-assisted tool — the doctor always makes the final diagnostic decision. AI findings are presented as suggestions with confidence scores. You review, confirm, modify, or delete each detection. You maintain full control.

**Q3: How accurate is the AI?**
A: Our internal validation shows 95% sensitivity for active bleeding, 90% for polyps, and 88% for ulcers. We are conducting external clinical trials to validate these metrics. AI performance varies by lesion type, image quality, and clinical context — which is why doctor review is essential.

**Q4: What if the AI misses a lesion?**
A: AI filtering is conservative — we prioritize recall over precision (better to flag too many than miss a critical finding). However, no AI is perfect. That's why you review filtered images and have access to all original frames. If you spot something AI missed, you can add it to the report manually.

**Q5: Is my patient data secure?**
A: Yes. All data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3). Cloud deployment uses AWS HIPAA-eligible services. On-premise deployment keeps data entirely on your servers. We maintain full audit logs of all PHI access. See our [Security Whitepaper] for details.

**Q6: How much does TheraSeus cost?**
A: Pay-per-use pricing is $135/case (no contract). Volume discounts available for practices doing 20+ cases/month ($99/case). Enterprise plans (on-premise, SSO, SLA) are custom-priced. Pilot program: first 50 cases FREE.

**Q7: What capsule endoscopes are supported?**
A: TheraSeus is device-agnostic. We support any capsule endoscope that outputs standard image formats (DICOM, JPEG, MP4). This includes Medtronic PillCam, Olympus EndoCapsule, IntroMedic MiroCam, Ankon NaviCam, and more.

**Q8: Can I use TheraSeus on-premise?**
A: Yes. On-premise deployment is available for hospitals with strict data residency requirements. Requires a GPU server (equivalent to AWS g4dn.xlarge). Contact us for hardware specs and installation support.

**Q9: Does TheraSeus integrate with my EMR/PACS?**
A: TheraSeus supports DICOM image import and HL7 FHIR report export. We have tested integration with Sectra, GE Centricity, Orion Health, and Epic. If you use a different system, contact us — we can likely integrate via standard protocols.

**Q10: How long is the pilot program?**
A: 3 months or 50 cases (whichever comes first). After the pilot, you can choose to continue with pay-per-use pricing, upgrade to a Practice or Enterprise plan, or discontinue (no obligations).

## Tone & Style Guide

**Voice**:
- Professional but approachable
- Clinician-focused (not overly technical for patients, not dumbed-down for doctors)
- Evidence-based (cite stats, studies, compliance standards)
- Transparent (acknowledge limitations, "in progress" vs. "approved")

**Avoid**:
- Overhyping AI ("revolutionize", "eliminate errors" — too strong)
- Medical jargon without context (assume reader is a doctor, but define abbreviations first use)
- Unsupported claims (don't say "best in class" without evidence)
- Fearmongering ("If you're still doing manual reviews, you're falling behind")

**Do**:
- Emphasize time savings (quantify where possible)
- Highlight doctor control (AI assists, doesn't replace)
- Use plain language for complex concepts
- Provide evidence (link to studies, show metrics)
